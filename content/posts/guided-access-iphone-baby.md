Guided Access is one of the most useful iPhone features that most people never turn on. It locks your phone to a single app and lets you disable the parts you don't want touched, which makes it a go-to trick for parents handing a device to a small child. This guide covers exactly how to set it up, how to use it well with a baby or toddler, and the honest limits you should know before relying on it.

## What Guided Access Actually Does

Guided Access pins your iPhone to one app. Once a session starts, the home swipe, app switcher, and (optionally) hardware buttons stop working. Your child can tap and swipe inside that one app, but they can't leave it, jump to your messages, or get to the App Store. To end the session, you enter a passcode or use Face ID or Touch ID.

It lives under Accessibility because it was originally designed for focus and learning support, but it works just as well for keeping little fingers contained.

## How to Set Up Guided Access

You only do this part once.

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Tap **Accessibility**.
3. Scroll down to **Guided Access** (under the General section) and tap it.
4. Toggle **Guided Access** on.
5. Tap **Passcode Settings**, then **Set Guided Access Passcode**, and enter a passcode you'll remember. Consider enabling Face ID or Touch ID here too so you can end sessions quickly.

That's it. The feature is now ready whenever you need it.

## How to Start a Session

1. Open the app you want to lock your child into.
2. **Triple-click the side button** (on iPhones with Face ID) or the **home button** (on older models).
3. The Guided Access screen appears. Here you can:
   - **Circle areas to disable.** Draw a circle around any button or region you want to make untappable, like an in-app menu or a purchase button.
   - **Tap Options** (bottom left) to toggle the Sleep/Wake button, Volume buttons, Motion, Keyboards, and Touch on or off.
4. Tap **Start** in the top right.

The phone is now locked to that app with your chosen restrictions.

## How to End a Session

Triple-click the side or home button again, then enter your Guided Access passcode (or use Face ID/Touch ID). Tap **End** in the top left. You're back to normal.

If you ever get stuck, holding the side and volume buttons and forcing a restart will exit Guided Access, though you'll rarely need that.

## Using Guided Access With a Baby or Toddler

A few practical tips make it work better with the youngest users.

- **Disable the hardware buttons.** In Options, turn off the Sleep/Wake and Volume buttons so your toddler can't lock the screen, blast the volume, or trigger the power-off slider.
- **Circle the risky zones.** If the app has a settings gear, a back arrow, or an ad in the corner, draw a circle around it so taps there do nothing.
- **Consider disabling Touch entirely** for a video. If you just want your child to watch something without pausing or skipping, turning off Touch in Options makes the screen playback-only. Great for Watch-Mode style calming.
- **Set a time limit.** In Options you can set a Time Limit, and Guided Access will announce when time is up, a soft way to end screen time.

## The Limits You Should Know

Guided Access is genuinely useful, but it's a general-purpose containment tool, not a baby product. Here's where it comes up short.

**It doesn't provide any content.** Guided Access only locks your child into whatever app is already open. If that app isn't truly baby-safe, has ads, has in-app purchases, or has content that isn't age-appropriate, your child is still interacting with all of it. Guided Access is a fence around a yard you have to build yourself.

**Setup takes a few seconds every single time.** Triple-click, optionally circle zones, open options, tap start. When your child is already fussing, that friction is real, and the triple-click can be finicky or forgotten. There's no true one-tap start.

**Accidental taps still register inside the app.** Unless you carefully circle every interactive element, a toddler mashing the screen can trigger things you didn't intend. Circling zones helps, but it's manual and easy to skip in a hurry.

**It's easy to leave in the wrong app.** Because it locks whatever's open, it's on you to remember to open the safe app first. Open the wrong one and your child is locked into, say, your email.

None of this makes Guided Access bad. It makes it a lock without a playground. It's ideal when you already have a specific safe app to lock into and you're willing to do the setup.

## FAQ

### How do I turn on Guided Access on my iPhone?
Go to Settings > Accessibility > Guided Access, toggle it on, then set a passcode under Passcode Settings. To use it, open an app and triple-click the side or home button, then tap Start.

### Why is my triple-click not starting Guided Access?
Make sure Guided Access is enabled in Settings > Accessibility and that you're triple-clicking the correct button (side button on Face ID iPhones, home button on older ones) at a steady pace. If Accessibility Shortcut has multiple features assigned, a menu may appear instead.

### Can Guided Access disable the volume and power buttons?
Yes. When you start a session, tap Options and toggle off the Sleep/Wake and Volume buttons so your child can't press them. This is highly recommended for toddlers.

### Does Guided Access make an app safe for my baby?
Not by itself. It only locks your child into whatever app is open. If that app has ads, purchases, or non-kid content, those are still reachable. Pair it with a genuinely baby-safe app for real safety.

### How do I get out of Guided Access if I forget the passcode?
Force restart the iPhone by pressing and quickly releasing volume up, then volume down, then holding the side button until the Apple logo appears. This exits Guided Access. Then reset your passcode in Settings.
