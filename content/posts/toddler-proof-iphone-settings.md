Toddlers treat a phone like a toy with infinite buttons, and every button does something. Left unprotected, a two-year-old can rack up an in-app purchase, delete photos, dial a contact, or change settings you didn't know existed. The good news: iOS has plenty of built-in guardrails. You just have to turn them on.

Here are seven settings that toddler-proof your iPhone, from quick toggles to full lockdowns. Set these up once and hand over your phone with a lot less anxiety.

## 1. Guided Access: Lock the Phone to One App

This is the big one. Guided Access pins your iPhone to a single app so your toddler can't swipe out to your messages, email, or the App Store.

Turn it on under **Settings > Accessibility > Guided Access**, set a passcode, then triple-click the side button inside any app to start a session. In Options you can disable the hardware buttons and even specific touch zones.

It's the single most effective toddler-proofing tool on the phone. Its main limit is that it only contains your child in whatever app is already open, so you still need a safe app to lock into. For a full walkthrough, see [Guided Access on iPhone: Complete Guide](/blog/guided-access-iphone-baby/).

## 2. Turn Off In-App Purchases and Require a Password

Nothing ruins a quiet moment like a surprise charge. Head to **Settings > Screen Time > Content & Privacy Restrictions**.

- Turn on **Content & Privacy Restrictions**.
- Under **iTunes & App Store Purchases**, set **In-app Purchases** to **Don't Allow**, and set **Installing Apps** and **Deleting Apps** to your preference.
- Set **Require Password** to **Always Require** for purchases.

This one setting prevents the classic horror story of a toddler buying a pile of coins in a game.

## 3. Set Up Screen Time Limits

Even with the best intentions, screen sessions creep longer than planned. **Settings > Screen Time** lets you set **App Limits** and a **Downtime** schedule so apps lock automatically after a set period.

For a toddler, a hard limit does two jobs: it caps exposure and it gives you a built-in, no-argument reason to end the session when the app locks.

## 4. Disable Notifications and Control Center Access

A toddler pulling down Control Center can toggle airplane mode, turn on the flashlight, or start a screen recording. And notification banners can open apps with a tap.

Under **Settings > Face ID & Passcode** (scroll to "Allow Access When Locked"), you can restrict Control Center and Notification Center on the lock screen. Combined with Guided Access during active play, this keeps the swipe-down surprises to a minimum.

## 5. Lock Down Deleting Apps and Changing Settings

Toddlers love to press and hold, and that's exactly how apps get deleted or rearranged. In **Settings > Screen Time > Content & Privacy Restrictions**:

- Set **Deleting Apps** to **Don't Allow**.
- Under **Allow Changes**, set **Account Changes**, **Cellular Data Changes**, and **Passcode Changes** to **Don't Allow**.

Now your child can't uninstall your banking app or flip settings you'll spend twenty minutes trying to find again.

## 6. Enable the Silent Switch and Volume Cap

Loud, sudden audio can startle a baby, and toddlers love cranking the volume. Set a **Volume Limit** and be mindful of the physical silent switch. If you use a purpose-built kids app, check that it respects the silent switch so a muted phone stays muted.

For headphones, **Settings > Sounds & Haptics > Headphone Safety** can cap loud audio, worth enabling for older kids too.

## Putting It Together

You don't need all seven every time. A practical baseline:

- **One-time setup:** Enable Guided Access (1), lock down purchases and deletions (2, 5), and set a volume cap (6).
- **Every session:** Open a kid-safe app (7), start Guided Access with hardware buttons off (1), and lean on the app's own timer or Screen Time limits (3).

Do that and the phone becomes a supervised toy instead of a minefield. For the two main ways to actually lock the screen, compare [How to Lock Your iPhone Screen for a Baby](/blog/lock-iphone-screen-for-baby/).

## FAQ

### How do I stop my toddler from buying things on my iPhone?
Go to Settings > Screen Time > Content & Privacy Restrictions, turn on restrictions, and set In-app Purchases to Don't Allow. Also set Require Password to Always Require so no purchase goes through without your approval.

### What's the fastest way to toddler-proof my phone?
Set up Guided Access once (Settings > Accessibility > Guided Access), then triple-click the side button inside a kid-safe app to lock the phone to it. That single feature blocks most of the trouble a toddler can cause.

### Can I stop my toddler from deleting my apps?
Yes. In Settings > Screen Time > Content & Privacy Restrictions, set Deleting Apps to Don't Allow. This prevents the press-and-hold-to-delete gesture from removing your apps.

### How do I limit screen time for a toddler automatically?
Use Settings > Screen Time to set App Limits and Downtime, which lock apps after a set period. A dedicated kids app with a built-in session timer adds a second automatic cutoff with a gentle wind-down.

### Is Guided Access enough on its own?
It locks the phone to one app, but it doesn't make that app safe. Combine it with purchase and deletion restrictions and a genuinely kid-safe app so there's nothing harmful reachable inside the locked session.
