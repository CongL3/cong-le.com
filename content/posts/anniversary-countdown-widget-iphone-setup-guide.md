A countdown you have to open an app to see is a countdown you will forget about. The whole point of a widget is to remove that step — to put your day count right on the home or lock screen so it greets you every time you pick up the phone. No tapping, no searching, just a passive glance that keeps the date top of mind.

This guide covers how iPhone widgets work, how to add one, and how to get the most out of an anniversary countdown specifically. The mechanics are the same across most widget-enabled apps, so once you learn the pattern you can place widgets confidently anywhere.

## Home screen vs lock screen: pick your surface

iPhone gives you two places to put a widget, and they suit different habits.

**Home screen widgets** are larger and can show more — a photo, a title, a big day count, extra context. They live among your app icons and are great if you unlock straight to your home screen and browse it often.

**Lock screen widgets** are small and glanceable, sitting above or below the clock. You see them *before* you even unlock, dozens of times a day, every time you check the time or a notification. Apple introduced home screen widgets in iOS 14 and added lock screen widgets in iOS 16, and iOS 17's StandBy mode turns a charging iPhone on its side into a full-screen glanceable display — all of which make a countdown one of the easiest things to keep permanently in view. For a countdown you want constantly in the corner of your eye, the lock screen is hard to beat.

You do not have to choose just one. Many people put a rich home screen widget for the detail and a tiny lock screen widget for the constant reminder.

## Adding a home screen widget, step by step

The flow is the same for any widget-enabled app:

1. **Touch and hold** an empty area of your home screen until the app icons jiggle.
2. Tap the **plus button** in the top corner to open the widget gallery.
3. **Search for the app** or scroll to find it in the list.
4. **Swipe** through the available sizes (small, medium, large) and stop on the one you want.
5. Tap **Add Widget**, then drag it wherever you like and tap **Done**.

If the widget supports it, touch and hold the placed widget and choose **Edit Widget** to configure what it shows — for example, which date it counts toward.

## Adding a lock screen widget, step by step

1. **Touch and hold** the lock screen (you may need to unlock with Face ID or your passcode first), then tap **Customize** and choose **Lock Screen**.
2. Tap the **widget area** — the box below the clock (or the line above it for the inline slot).
3. **Select the app** from the gallery, then tap or drag its widget into an open slot.
4. Tap **Done** and set it as your wallpaper pair if prompted.

Lock screen real estate is tight, so these widgets show a compact version — usually just the essential number. That is exactly what you want for an at-a-glance countdown.

## Troubleshooting a widget that won't cooperate

If your widget shows the wrong date or looks stale, work through the usual suspects:

- **Nothing appears in the gallery?** Open the app once first — widgets often only register after the app has launched at least once. Confirm the app is fully installed and updated.
- **Wrong date showing?** Touch and hold the widget, choose Edit Widget, and reselect the correct date or configuration.
- **Count looks frozen?** Open the app to let it refresh, and make sure Background App Refresh is enabled for it in Settings so the widget updates on its own.
- **Widget option missing entirely?** Check whether widgets require the app's Pro tier and whether your version supports them.

## FAQ

### How do I add an anniversary widget to my iPhone home screen?

Touch and hold an empty area of the home screen until the icons jiggle, tap the plus button, find the app in the widget gallery, swipe to pick a size, and tap Add Widget. Then touch and hold the placed widget and choose Edit Widget to set which date it counts.

### Can I put an anniversary countdown on my lock screen?

Yes. Touch and hold the lock screen, tap Customize, choose Lock Screen, tap the widget slot below the clock, and select the app's widget. Lock screen widgets are small and glanceable, which is ideal for a countdown you want to see constantly.

### Why isn't my widget showing the right number of days?

Most often the date configuration or the Day 1 setting is off. In Anniversary Tracker you can choose whether Day 1 starts on your anniversary or the day after. Touch and hold the widget, choose Edit Widget, and confirm the correct date is selected.

### Do anniversary widgets require a paid version?

Sometimes. Widgets are a Pro-tier feature in some apps, so check the app's listing or settings if the widget option does not appear. Confirm the feature is available before you build your daily-glance workflow around it.

### My widget won't appear in the gallery. What now?

Open the app at least once — widgets usually register only after the first launch — and make sure the app is fully installed and updated. Enabling Background App Refresh in Settings also helps the widget stay current on its own.
