Every wedding anniversary has a traditional name — paper, cotton, silver, gold — and behind each is a bit of symbolism about how a marriage is meant to be growing at that point. The custom of assigning materials to anniversary years goes back generations, and while modern lists have added their own alternatives, the traditional names below are the well-established ones you will find echoed across long-standing sources.

This is your reference for what each milestone is called, what the material is meant to suggest, and a sensible gift direction for each. Use it to plan ahead, and consider tracking the ones that matter to you so the big years never sneak up.

## The early years: building the foundation

The first several anniversaries are traditionally marked with humble, everyday materials. That is the point — early marriage is delicate and still taking shape, and the materials grow sturdier as the years go on.

| Year | Traditional name | The idea | Gift direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Paper | Blank, full of potential; a fresh page | A letter, a book, tickets, framed art |
| 2 | Cotton | Comfortable, interwoven, a little stronger | Soft linens, cozy clothing |
| 3 | Leather | Durable, protective, hard-wearing | A wallet, bag, journal, or belt |
| 4 | Fruit or Flowers | Blossoming and bearing fruit | Flowers, a fruit tree, a garden day |
| 5 | Wood | Strong, rooted, established | A wooden keepsake, a plant, furniture |

By the fifth year the symbolism has shifted from fragile paper to solid wood — a nod to a partnership that has taken root.

## Building strength: years six through fifteen

The middle years lean into metals and increasingly refined materials, reflecting a marriage that has proven its durability and is being polished by time.

| Year | Traditional name | Gift direction |
|---|---|---|
| 6 | Iron | Ironwork, a cast pan, sturdy tools |
| 7 | Wool | A blanket, sweater, or warm throw |
| 8 | Bronze | A bronze ornament or sculpture |
| 9 | Pottery | Handmade ceramics, a pottery class |
| 10 | Tin | Tin-accented keepsakes, or start leaning modern |
| 11 | Steel | Steel jewelry, a quality tool or watch |
| 12 | Silk | Silk scarf, tie, or bedding |
| 13 | Lace | Lace clothing or delicate detailing |
| 14 | Ivory | Ivory-colored keepsakes (choose ethical, faux materials) |
| 15 | Crystal | Crystal glassware or a decorative piece |

A note on the fourteenth year: traditional lists name ivory, but real ivory is rightly off the table today. The graceful move is to honor the *color* and spirit with cream-toned or faux-ivory pieces rather than the material itself.

## The major milestones: the ones everyone knows

After fifteen years the tradition spaces things out to every five years, and the materials become genuinely precious. These are the anniversaries that get parties.

- **20 — China.** Fine, beautiful, and enduring when cared for. A tea set or fine dinnerware.
- **25 — Silver.** The first "big" milestone. Silver jewelry, a keepsake, or a silver-themed celebration.
- **30 — Pearl.** Formed slowly, layer by layer, over years — a fitting image for three decades. Pearl jewelry.
- **35 — Coral.** Something built patiently over a very long time.
- **40 — Ruby.** Deep, warm, and rare. Ruby jewelry is the classic choice.
- **45 — Sapphire.** A jewel of loyalty and wisdom.
- **50 — Gold.** The golden anniversary, and the one most people aim their whole marriage toward. Anything gold, and usually a proper celebration.
- **55 — Emerald.**
- **60 — Diamond.** The hardest, most enduring stone for the most enduring marriages — famously associated with sixty years.
- **70 — Platinum.** Rare and precious, for a truly remarkable span.

The through-line from paper to platinum tells a small story on its own: a marriage that starts as a blank page and, decade by decade, becomes something as rare and hard-wearing as any material there is.

## FAQ

### What are the traditional wedding anniversary names by year?

The best-established traditional names run paper (1), cotton (2), leather (3), fruit or flowers (4), and wood (5) in the early years, through silver (25), pearl (30), ruby (40), gold (50), and diamond (60). Each material symbolizes the growing strength of a marriage.

### Why is the 14th anniversary called ivory if ivory is banned?

The traditional name predates modern conservation law. Today the respectful approach is to honor the color and spirit of the milestone with cream-toned or faux-ivory pieces rather than genuine ivory, which should never be purchased.

### What is the difference between traditional and modern anniversary lists?

Traditional lists assign the long-standing materials shown here. Modern lists, developed later, offer alternative gifts for many years. Both are widely used; the traditional names are the older, more universally recognized set.

### How far ahead should I plan an anniversary gift?

For the milestone years that call for jewelry, fine china, or a commissioned keepsake, give yourself a few weeks — those often need ordering ahead. Setting a reminder well before the date keeps a big anniversary from becoming a last-minute scramble.

### Do I have to wait every five years to celebrate?

No. Day counts and monthly anniversaries give you smaller occasions all year round. Many couples mark 100, 500, and 1,000 days together in addition to the yearly milestones, which keeps the celebrating going between the big anniversaries.
