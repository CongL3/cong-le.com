Starting a new career in [Hoop Quest](/apps/hoop-quest/) is a bit like standing at the top of a long staircase you cannot see the bottom of. You create a player, pick a position, and suddenly there are ratings, money, popularity, trust, a draft board, a whole generated league world moving around you. Where do you even begin?

This guide walks you through the early game so your first save is not the one you throw away. The good news: Hoop Quest keeps your career data on the device, needs no account for core play, and lets you sim quickly, so experimenting is cheap. The better news is that a few habits early on compound into a much stronger legend later.

## Step 1: Build a player with a plan, not a vibe

When you create your player you choose a position, jersey number, and portrait style. The portrait and number are flavor — pick whatever you like. The position is a real decision, because it shapes which ratings matter most to your career.

Before you commit, ask yourself what kind of career you want to *tell the story of*. A player built around scoring will chase different awards and news moments than one built around all-around play. There is no wrong answer, but there is a difference between drifting into a position and choosing one on purpose. A chosen identity gives every later decision a clear yardstick: "does this help the player I am trying to become?"

## Step 2: Treat age-by-age choices as investments

The heart of Hoop Quest is making age-by-age choices that affect **ratings, money, popularity, and trust**. This is where beginners lose the most ground, because it is tempting to grab whatever gives the biggest immediate number.

Think of those four meters as different currencies:

- **Ratings** are your on-court ceiling. They decide how you actually perform in live games.
- **Money** buys career upgrades later. Underrated early because the payoff is delayed.
- **Popularity** is your public profile — it colors the news stories and moments your career generates.
- **Trust** is your relationship capital within your program and situation.

You cannot max all four at once, and trying to will leave you mediocre at everything. Pick the one or two that serve the player identity you chose in Step 1, and let the others grow more slowly. A scorer who protects ratings and popularity will feel very different from an all-arounder who banks money and trust — and both are valid paths to legend.

## Step 3: Pick a school program that fits your build

Early on you choose a school program and grow through campus seasons before chasing the draft board. When you weigh fictional teams, you are looking at differences in **tempo, defense, prestige, and play style**.

A quick way to think about it:

- **Prestige** raises your visibility and the stakes, but a bigger stage can be harder to stand out on right away.
- **Tempo and play style** determine how many opportunities flow your way and whether they suit your build. A fast, offense-minded team creates more scoring chances; a defense-first team rewards a different kind of player.
- **Defense** shapes the kind of games you will grind through.

Match the program to your player. A high-tempo scorer thrives somewhere that pushes the pace. If you picked a grittier build, a defense-oriented program lets your strengths show. Chasing the highest-prestige option regardless of fit is a classic rookie mistake.

## Step 4: Play the games that matter, sim the ones that don't

Hoop Quest gives you two speeds. You can **play possession-by-possession live games** with box scores and play-by-play, or **sim full weeks** to move through long seasons quickly. Knowing when to use each is half of playing well.

Play out the games that carry weight — the ones where a strong box score moves your ratings, popularity, or a career moment forward, and where you want to influence the result directly. Sim the filler so seasons do not drag. Early on, lean toward playing more games manually so you learn how your build performs; once you understand your player, sim more aggressively to reach the milestones you are chasing. The rhythm of *play the big ones, sim the rest* is what keeps a long career from becoming a grind.

## Step 5: Manage stamina and spend on the right upgrades

Two systems quietly decide whether your prime is spectacular or merely fine: **stamina** and **career upgrades**.

Manage stamina like a resource, not an afterthought. Running your player into the ground blunts performance in exactly the games you most want to win. Give yourself room to be sharp when it counts.

For upgrades, this is where that "boring" money from Step 2 pays off. Spend on training and upgrades that reinforce your identity rather than patching every weakness. A specialist who is elite at one thing generates more standout moments — and a stronger legacy score — than a generalist who is fine at everything.

## Step 6: Build legacy across every stage

The endgame of Hoop Quest is **legacy** — local legacy scores that build across the school, campus, and top-tier career stages. Follow the news stories, draft prospects, standings, and awards, and treat each stage as a chapter that adds to the whole, not a hurdle to rush past.

The players who become all-time legends are not the ones who min-maxed a single season. They are the ones who made consistent, identity-driven choices at every age, protected their stamina, spent money with intent, and let the generated world's awards and moments accumulate. Do that and the climb from rookie to MVP stops feeling like a staircase in the dark and starts feeling like a story you are writing on purpose.

## FAQ

### What position should I pick as a beginner in Hoop Quest?

Pick the position that matches the career story you want to tell, then build your ratings around it. There is no single "best" position — the key is choosing one deliberately so every later decision has a clear standard to measure against.

### How do the age-by-age choices work?

Each choice nudges some combination of your ratings, money, popularity, and trust. Treat them as investments in different currencies rather than grabbing the biggest number each time, and prioritize the meters that serve your chosen player identity.

### Should I play every game or sim them?

Play out the games that matter for your progression, especially early while you are learning your build, and sim the filler to keep seasons moving. As you get comfortable, sim more aggressively to reach milestones faster.

### How do I build a strong legacy score?

Make consistent, identity-driven choices across the school, campus, and top-tier stages, manage your stamina so you perform when it counts, and spend money on upgrades that reinforce your strengths. Legacy rewards a coherent long career, not one min-maxed season.

### Do I need an internet connection to play?

No. Hoop Quest requires no account for core play and stores your career data on the device, so you can build your legend offline.
