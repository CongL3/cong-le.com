Birthdays and wedding anniversaries get all the attention, but they are a tiny slice of the dates that actually shape a life. The day you moved into your first apartment. The morning you brought the dog home. The year you quit something that was hurting you. These moments matter just as much — we just rarely write them down, so they blur together and get lost.

Here are fifty dates worth tracking, organized by theme. You will not want all of them, but scanning the list is a reliable way to remember the ones you *do* care about and would kick yourself for forgetting. Anything on here can live in a single tracker so it resurfaces at the right time every year.

## Relationship and love milestones

The obvious ones, plus the quieter dates that tell the fuller story.

1. Your first date
2. Your first kiss
3. The day you became official
4. The day you said "I love you"
5. The day you moved in together
6. Your engagement day
7. Your wedding day
8. The day you first met (not always the first date)
9. A yearly "renewal" date you set for just the two of you
10. The anniversary of a memorable trip you took together

## Family and children

11. Each child's birthday

14. The day you brought a baby home
15. First steps or first words (approximate is fine)
16. Adoption day or "gotcha day"
17. A grandparent's or parent's birthday
18. The anniversary of a loved one you have lost
19. The day the family moved to a new home
20. A yearly family reunion or tradition date

## Pets

## FAQ

### What dates should I track besides birthdays and anniversaries?

Consider first dates, the day you moved in together, adoption or "gotcha" days for pets, sobriety and recovery milestones, work anniversaries, the day you bought your home, and countdowns to events you are excited about. Any date that carries meaning is worth recording.

### Can I track non-romantic dates in an anniversary app?

Yes. Anniversary Tracker is explicitly built for more than couples — pet adoption days, an idol's birthday, an upcoming exam, or any special event all belong in it. If the date matters to you, you can add and count it.

### What are "gotcha day" and sobriety milestones?

A "gotcha day" is the anniversary of the day a pet or an adopted child joined your family. Sobriety milestones mark time since someone stopped drinking or using — commonly celebrated at intervals like 30 days, 90 days, and one year. Both are meaningful dates many people track yearly.

### How do day-count milestones work?

Instead of only counting years, a day counter shows exactly how many days have passed and flags round-number milestones like 100, 500, and 1,000 days. Anniversary Tracker generates these automatically, giving you more occasions to celebrate between the yearly dates.
