Built on proven solunar theory

Solunar theory, introduced by John Alden Knight in 1926, is the most trusted method anglers use to predict peak feeding activity. Fish bite most aggressively when the moon is directly overhead (transit) or underfoot (anti-transit). Moon phase and lunar distance further refine each day's score.

Frequently asked questions

What is solunar theory?

Introduced by John Alden Knight in 1926, solunar theory predicts peak feeding activity. Fish bite most aggressively when the moon is directly overhead (transit) or underfoot (anti-transit); moon phase and lunar distance refine each day's score.

Does it work without internet?

Yes. Every calculation runs on your device with pure astronomical maths. It works on the boat with no signal, at the lake at dawn, and in airplane mode — no server, no expiring API key, no "data unavailable".

What does the 1–5 fish rating mean?

It answers "should I fish today?" in one second using 1 to 5 fish icons, factoring in moon transit and anti-transit timing, moon phase, and lunar distance for that day.

What are major and minor feeding windows?

Major windows are 2-hour peak periods centered on moon transit and anti-transit; minor windows are 1-hour periods centered on moonrise and moonset. Both appear on a 24-hour timeline over sunrise and sunset bands.

What does Solunar Fishing Pro add?

A 30-day calendar, home and lock screen widgets, up to 5 saved locations, and notification customisation. Pro is monthly or annual, and existing one-time purchasers keep lifetime access after restoring.

Is my location private?

Yes. Your location is used only for on-device computation and is never transmitted anywhere. There are no accounts, and no internet is required for any feature.
